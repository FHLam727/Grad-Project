How Heat Score Is Calculated

Heat Score Formula

The final heat score is a weighted combination of four log-scaled indicator components.

Heat Score = 0.45 × Engagement Factor + 0.25 × Discussion Factor + 0.15 × Diversity Factor + 0.15 × Velocity Factor

Metric Definitions

Engagement

Total likes, comments, and shares linked to the cluster. Higher engagement means the conversation is producing stronger audience response.

Engagement Factor = log1p(Total Likes + Total Comments + Total Shares)
Discussion

Total discussion volume inside the selected time window. This includes cluster-linked post discussion plus captured comment and sub-comment activity when those fields are available from the source platform.

Discussion Factor = log1p(Comment Count + Fetched Comment Count + Fetched Sub-comment Count)
Diversity

Distinct authors contributing to the cluster. Higher diversity means the topic is spreading beyond a small number of accounts.

Diversity Factor = log1p(Count of Distinct Authors)
Velocity

Posting speed inside the selected window. Fast daily post growth increases momentum and helps surface emerging topics.

Velocity Factor = log1p(Recent Post Count Within 24 Hours)

How To Read The Score

8.5 - 10.0

Very high heat. Strong engagement, wide discussion, and clear momentum across multiple indicators.

7.0 - 8.5

High heat. A clearly active topic with strong public attention, but slightly less balanced than the top tier.

5.0 - 7.0

Moderate heat. Visible conversation or engagement exists, but the signal is not yet dominant across every indicator.

Below 5.0

Early or limited heat. The topic may still be niche, fragmented, or waiting for stronger discussion and momentum.

How ranking works

The leaderboard source dropdown changes the ordering logic for the table, but the heat score itself still follows the same formula. This lets users compare events by one driver without changing the underlying heat calculation.

Worked example

If the four normalized components are 8.8, 9.2, 7.4, and 8.0, the weighted heat score is:

0.45 × 8.8 + 0.25 × 9.2 + 0.15 × 7.4 + 0.15 × 8.0 = 8.57
