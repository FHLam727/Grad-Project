How Heat Score Works
1
Per-post Engagement Score — PCA-weighted log engagement (likes, comments, shares, views) scaled by account followers. post_score = PCA(log1p(engagement)) × log1p(followers)
2
Event Group Score — posts about the same event are deduplicated into a group. Score = average post score × log(post count + 1). raw = avg(post_scores) × log1p(n_posts)
3
Time Decay — recency matters. Heat decays with a 14-day half-life from the newest post date. decay = exp(−ln2 / 14 × days_since_newest)
4
Normalise 0–100 — log-compress extreme values, then min-max scale all events. heat = minmax(log1p(raw × decay)) × 100
5
AI Activity Aggregation — one AI-extracted activity may span multiple event groups. Final heat = source-count–weighted average of constituent group scores.

PCA weights are re-fitted from live data each time heat_analyzer.py runs. Scores are cached in the DB — use Refresh AI Analysis to rebuild.
